How To Play

This is a matching pairs game to help you learn and revise Specialised Cells and their Functions.

A specialised cell is any cell that has developed features to perform a specific job.

Match the 'Cell Type' images with their 'Function' descriptions.

You can read about the different cell types from the game here:

Cell Type Function

Ciliated Epithelial Cell		 This cell uses tiny hairs to trap bacteria and dust in air passages.

Egg Cell		 This cell fertilises with a sperm then develops into an embryo.

Muscle Cell		 This cell shortens and lengthens, allowing animals to move.

Nerve Cell		 This cell carries electrical signals to the brain and body.

Palisade Cell		 This cell helps plants do photosynthesis to make food from sunlight

Red Blood Cell		 This cell transports oxygen around the body.

Root Hair Cell		 This plant cell absorbs water and minerals from soil.

Sperm Cell		 This cell fuses with an egg cell for fertilisation to occur.
